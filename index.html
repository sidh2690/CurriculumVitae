Current Projects

Key Science Paper for Southern Wide-Field Gamma-Ray Observatory (SWGO)

I am currently collaborating with Dr. Ekrem Oğuzhan Angüner and his team on the development of a key science paper that specifically explores the phenomenon of peVatrons within the context of the Southern Wide-Field Gamma-Ray Observatory (SWGO). Our research is dedicated to shedding light on the mechanisms and astrophysical environments related to particles reaching petaelectronvolt (PeV) energies, as observed by the SWGO. This work holds significant implications for understanding cosmic-ray acceleration and the intricate processes within the universe's most energetic phenomena.

Classification of Noises in Gravitational Waves

I am actively engaged in a project that entails the classification of noises within gravitational wave data from LIGO O3b. Our research revolves around the application of complex few-shot meta-learning models in the domain of machine learning. The primary goal of this research is to significantly enhance the precision and reliability of gravitational wave data analysis by effectively identifying and categorizing glitches. By doing so, we aim to contribute to the continuous refinement of gravitational wave detection techniques, thereby advancing our comprehension of the cosmos.

CortexFlow: Machine Learning Library

In collaboration with colleagues, actively involved in the development of the CortexFlow machine learning library. This library is designed to offer a versatile set of tools for tasks such as data preprocessing, model testing, and more. CortexFlow aims to streamline and enhance the machine learning workflow, contributing to the advancement of data science and artificial intelligence applications.
